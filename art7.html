Introduction:

Duolingo is an incredibly popular language learning app. It started in 2009 and publicly launched in 2012. Duolingo is widely known for tracking players’ streaks -- the number of consecutive days that the app is visited. This game creates a competitive environment by using leagues (ranks) that players are put in based on points they earn while playing. This pits players against each other in a race of learning. Unlike previous games, Duolingo uses this competitive feature to fuel a social aspect which encourages the users to play more. Furthermore, unlike the previous games, Duolingo uses AI algorithms to match the user's learning pace.

Educational Value

Duolingo is a language learning app, where users are offered 40 different languages to learn from (as of June 2024). Not only can players learn languages from around the world but languages from pop-culture fandoms (Klingon and Dothraki) are also available to be learned. According to Blue Ocean, “Duolingo learners reached comparable levels of reading and listening proficiency to university students”(Blue Ocean Contributors, 2024).

The Legacy

Duolingo has grown into one of the most popular educational games presently. Users all over the globe use this app. As of 2023, it totaled over 500 million registered users (Wallingford, 2024). Not only is the number of users impressive, but the game has amassed a value of over 17 billion dollars as of February 25, 2025 (Duolingo Net Worth 2020-2024 | DUOL, 2020).
